The Science Behind FLASH

The CAMP Framework

A data-driven methodology that transforms startup evaluation from guesswork into science

From Art to Science

Traditional startup evaluation relies on gut feelings and incomplete data. The CAMP Framework changes that by quantifying the key drivers of startup success.

Developed through analysis of thousands of funding outcomes, CAMP evaluates startups across four critical dimensions that VCs care about most: Capital, Advantage, Market, and People.

Each pillar is weighted differently based on your startup's stage, ensuring you get relevant insights whether you're pre-seed or Series A.

The Four Pillars in Detail

What VCs actually evaluate when making funding decisions

Capital

Financial Health & Efficiency

The Capital pillar evaluates how efficiently you manage money and whether you've built investor confidence. It's not just about how much you've raisedâ€”it's about what you've done with it.

VCs want to see disciplined growth, not reckless burning. Strong capital metrics show you can turn investment into value, making you a safer bet for their next check.

Key Metrics

Funding history & investor quality
Monthly burn rate & runway
Burn multiple & efficiency
Capital structure & flexibility

Advantage

Competitive Moats & Differentiation

The Advantage pillar assesses your sustainable competitive advantages and barriers to entry. It answers the critical question: "Why can't someone else just copy this?"

In competitive markets, sustainable advantages determine long-term winners. VCs invest in companies that can defend their position and maintain pricing power as they scale.

Key Metrics

Patents & proprietary technology
Network effects & lock-in
Data advantages & insights
Brand strength & switching costs

Market

Opportunity Size & Traction

The Market pillar analyzes your total addressable market, growth dynamics, and current traction. It validates whether you're fishing in a big enough pond.

Great teams in small markets struggle to scale. VCs need to see not just a problem worth solving, but one that affects enough people to build a billion-dollar business.

Key Metrics

TAM, SAM, and SOM analysis
Market & user growth rates
Customer retention & satisfaction
Competitive landscape analysis

People

Team Capability & Execution

The People pillar evaluates your team's composition, experience, and ability to execute. It's often the most important factor in early-stage investing.

Ideas are commodities; execution is everything. VCs bet on teams that can navigate uncertainty, iterate quickly, and build something extraordinary.

Key Metrics

Team composition & skill gaps
Domain expertise & experience
Track record & previous exits
Advisory network quality
